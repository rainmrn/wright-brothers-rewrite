The Story of
Wright Brothers

Orville Wright (August 19, 1871 – January 30, 1948) and Wilbur Wright (April 16, 1867 – May 30, 1912), together known as the Wright brothers, were American aviation pioneers generally credited with inventing, building, and flying the world's first successful motor-operated airplane. The brothers were also the first to invent aircraft controls that made fixed-wing powered flight possible. Where do they come from, and how did they come to be?
